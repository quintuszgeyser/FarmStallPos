System Architecture

Farm Stall POS is made up of four services that run together on a single Mini PC (MINISFORUM UN1245 - Intel i5-12450H, 16 GB RAM). All services start automatically on boot.

POS Application

Flask web app (Python). Serves the browser UI and handles all business logic - sales, stock, customers, stats. Port 5000.

PostgreSQL Database

Stores all data: products, transactions, stock batches, customers, face embeddings. Two databases - farm_pos (QA) and farm_pos_prod (Production).

Frigate NVR

Camera monitoring software (Docker). Watches 2 cameras, detects people and cars using OpenVINO on the Intel GPU. Sends events to the Recognition Service. Port 8971.

Recognition Service

Python service that receives camera events from Frigate, extracts face / gait / plate biometrics, matches against customer database, and auto-enrolls new visitors. Port 8080.

Sessions & Auto-Logout All roles

The system tracks login activity automatically for staff time management.

  • Idle 10 minutes: the current activity session closes for time-tracking purposes. The user stays logged in - no interruption.
  • Idle 2 hours: automatic logout. The user must log in again.

This means a teller can step away for 20 minutes between customers without being logged out, but will be logged out automatically at end of day.

Product Dropdown

Search Bar

USB / Bluetooth Barcode Scanner

  1. Be on the Teller tab with no text input focused (click an empty area if needed).
  2. Scan. The screen flashes green and the item is added.

Camera Scanner & Cart Teller + Admin

Camera Barcode Scanner

  1. Click Scan to activate the camera.
  2. Point at the barcode. Item is added automatically with a 1.5-second cooldown between scans.
  3. Click Stop to turn off the camera.

Cart Management

  • Adjust quantity: use + / − on each cart line.
  • Remove an item: click Remove on the cart line.
  • Undo last scan: click ↩ Undo at the top of the cart.
  • Per-item discount (admin only): click the % button on a cart line.
  • Cart-wide discount (admin only): click % Discount above the cart.

Weight & Volume Items Teller + Admin

  1. Add the product. The Weight Entry modal opens automatically.
  2. Enter the quantity and select the unit (g, kg, ml, L, or a package unit).
  3. The live price preview updates as you type.
  4. Click Add to Cart.

Automatic Specials Teller + Admin

Specials (combo deals) detect and apply automatically every time the cart changes. The system finds the combination that saves the customer the most money. Each product unit counts toward at most one special. A toast notification announces the special when it applies.

Customising Recipes Teller + Admin

  1. Add a recipe product (e.g., cappuccino, toasted sandwich) to the cart.
  2. Click Customise on that cart line.
  3. Swap ingredients, remove them, or add extras from the available list.
  4. A price adjustment is shown based on ingredient cost differences.
  5. Click Add to Cart. The cart line shows a summary of all changes.

Checkout Teller + Admin

  1. Confirm the cart items and total.
  2. If a customer was detected at the till, their name and visit count show on screen. The sale will be linked to them automatically.
  3. Click Checkout.
  4. Stock is deducted (FIFO), kitchen orders are created for prepared items, and the cart clears.
Important Once checked out, only an admin can edit or void the transaction. Double-check the cart before checking out.

Archiving & Restoring Admin

Archiving

  2. If the product has remaining stock, choose: Keep stock (stays on Archived tab, re-usable) or Write off all remaining stock (permanently removed, cost logged).

Receiving Stock Admin

  1. Find the product and click Receive.
  2. Enter the quantity received. If a purchase package is configured (e.g., 5 L carton) you can enter "3 cartons" instead of "15 000 ml" - the system converts automatically.
  3. Enter the total amount paid (R). The cost per unit is shown live in friendly units (e.g., R8.33/L).

  5. Click Confirm - Receive Stock. A new FIFO batch is created; future sales consume older batches first.

Stocktake (Physical Count) Admin

  1. Click Stocktake on a product card.

Write-Off (Spoilage / Damage) Admin

Caution Write-offs permanently remove stock and record a cost loss. Use only for genuinely spoiled or damaged goods.
  1. Click Write Off on a product card.
  2. Enter the quantity and unit. The estimated FIFO cost consumed is shown live.
  3. Enter a reason (required) - e.g., Milk expired 2026-04-25.
  4. Click Confirm Write-Off.

The loss appears on the Stats tab. To correct an error, find the entry in Adjustment History at the bottom of the Products tab and click ✏️.

Recognition System - How It Works

The recognition system uses three types of biometric signal to identify customers:

SignalTechnologyRuns onWeight
FaceInsightFace (SCRFD detector + ArcFace embedding, 512-dim)Intel Iris Xe GPU (OpenVINO)Highest (6 pts)
GaitMediaPipe Pose (body proportions + temporal cadence analysis)CPULow (1 pt)
License platefast-plate-ocr (MobileViT OCR model)CPUSupport only (2 pts)

A customer is identified when the combined score across all available signals exceeds the link threshold. Physical attributes (height, build, hair colour) and visit patterns contribute smaller supporting scores.

Detection Pipeline

  1. Camera event: Frigate detects a person or car and sends a webhook to the Recognition Service.
  2. Snapshot fetch: the Recognition Service downloads the Frigate snapshot image.
  3. Signal extraction:
    • Face: SCRFD detects face in the head region, ArcFace extracts a 512-dim embedding. CLAHE contrast enhancement applied to low-light images.
    • Gait: MediaPipe Pose extracts 33 body landmarks, computes 6 body proportion features plus 12-dim temporal cadence from the video clip.
    • Plate (outdoor camera only): fast-plate-ocr reads any visible license plate.
  4. Matching: signals are scored against all enrolled customers in-memory (signal cache, no database query per event). Best match is selected.
  5. Track update: the person's track accumulates evidence over multiple camera events. Once confidence exceeds the link threshold, the customer is considered identified.
  6. Teller notification: the POS teller screen shows a greeting card with the customer's name, visit count, and top products hint. The next sale is linked to this customer.
  7. Clip analysis: when the camera event ends, a video clip is queued for background processing. Up to 30 frames are sampled to collect additional face angles (up to 24 distinct embeddings per customer) and temporal gait features.
CPU optimisation Face models (SCRFD + ArcFace) run on the Intel Iris Xe GPU via OpenVINO. MediaPipe Pose runs on CPU. The system uses separate concurrency controls so GPU and CPU inference can run in parallel - 2 concurrent GPU streams, 3 concurrent CPU gait sessions. Real-time gait is skipped when face quality is already ≥ 0.65, saving CPU.

Enrollment

Automatic Enrollment

The recognition service automatically creates a new customer record when it observes an unknown person for long enough:

  • Requires at least 5 face embeddings and 2 high-quality faces
  • The person must have been visible for at least 30 seconds
  • The best face similarity to any existing customer must be below 0.35 (preventing accidental duplicates)
  • The new customer gets an auto-generated number (e.g., C042) and no name until an admin assigns one

Manual Enrollment

Admins can manually create customers and link biometric data from the Customers tab. The recognition service will use these enrollments for future detections.

Customer Merge

Over time, the same person may have multiple records (auto-enrolled from different angles, or a manual record + an auto-enrolled one). The system detects these automatically.

Manual Review Panel

Pairs with similarity ≥ 0.55 (configurable) appear in the Merge Suggestions panel on the Customers tab. An admin reviews each suggestion and clicks Merge or Dismiss.

Auto-Merge

Pairs with similarity ≥ 0.95 (configurable) are merged automatically without requiring admin action.

What Merge Does

  • All face embeddings, gait features, license plates, visits, and sales from the secondary customer are moved to the primary customer
  • The secondary customer is marked as merged and hidden from the list
  • The primary customer is chosen automatically (higher score wins: visit count, has name, has face, has gait, older first-seen date)
  • Unmerge is available from the customer's merge history if needed

Till Detection

When a customer is identified by the indoor camera, the Teller tab shows a greeting card with:

  • Customer name (or "Customer C042" if unnamed)
  • Visit number (e.g., "Visit #12")
  • Top 3 products they usually buy

The greeting card appears automatically - no teller action needed. The next checkout will link the sale to this customer's profile, building their purchase history and visit analytics.

The till detection polls every 3 seconds while the Teller tab is active and stops when the tab is hidden to save bandwidth.
